1. Overview

TrailGuide Canvas AI Tutor ("TrailGuide," "we," "us," or "the Extension") is a browser extension for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge that provides AI-powered tutoring tools within the Canvas Learning Management System (LMS). This Privacy Policy explains what information the Extension collects, how it is used, and what choices you have.

By installing or using TrailGuide you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please uninstall the Extension.

2. What Information We Collect

2.1 Canvas Page Content

When you activate a TrailGuide feature (e.g., "Explain This," "Generate Study Guide," "Quiz Me"), the Extension reads the text content of the Canvas page you are currently viewing — such as assignment instructions, discussion posts, or course pages. This content is sent to the AI provider you have configured (see Section 4) to generate a response.

Key point Content is sent to the AI API only when you explicitly trigger a feature. The Extension does not continuously monitor or transmit your Canvas activity in the background.

2.2 API Keys

To use AI features, you provide one or more API keys for supported AI providers (Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, OpenAI). These keys are:

  • Stored locally in your browser using chrome.storage.local
  • Encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM using a key derived from a user-chosen password via PBKDF2
  • Never transmitted to TrailGuide servers (we operate no backend servers)
  • Sent directly from your browser to the respective AI provider's API endpoint when making requests

2.3 Chat History

Conversation history with the AI tutor is stored locally in your browser (chrome.storage.local). If you enable Google Drive sync, chat history is uploaded to your personal Google Drive account (see Section 2.6).

2.4 Learning & Study Data

The Extension stores study activity locally, including flashcard progress, quiz results, study timer sessions, and concept map data. This data is used to personalize your experience and is never sent to TrailGuide's servers. It may be synced to your Google Drive if you opt in.

2.5 Extension Settings

Your preferences — such as selected AI provider, voice character, and display options — are stored in chrome.storage.sync so they are available across your Chrome-signed-in devices. No settings are transmitted to TrailGuide.

2.6 Google Drive Sync (Optional, Opt-In Only)

If you choose to enable Google Drive integration, the Extension will request permission to access a limited scope of your Google Drive. Specifically, the Extension uses:

  • drive.file — read/write access only to files the Extension itself creates
  • drive.appdata — access to a hidden app-specific folder in your Drive
  • userinfo.email and userinfo.profile — to display your account name and email in the settings page

The Extension syncs the following data to your Google Drive: settings, chat history, learning progress, analytics, and course index data. This data is stored in your own Google Drive account, not on our servers. You can disconnect Drive sync at any time from the Extension's Options page, which will stop future uploads. Previously synced files remain in your Drive unless you manually delete them.

2.7 Voice Input

Voice features use the browser's built-in Web Speech API. Audio captured by your microphone is processed by your browser (and, depending on your browser's implementation, may be sent to the browser vendor's speech recognition service — see your browser's own privacy policy). TrailGuide never receives, records, or stores audio data.

2.8 On-Device AI (WebLLM)

If you configure TrailGuide to use an on-device AI model via WebLLM, the AI model runs entirely inside your browser using your device's GPU. No data is sent to any external server when using this mode. Model files are downloaded from Hugging Face the first time you use the feature and cached locally.

2.9 Data We Do NOT Collect

TrailGuide does not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or any personally identifying information (unless you voluntarily grant Google OAuth access, in which case we display but do not store your profile information beyond the session)
  • Browsing history outside of Canvas LMS domains
  • Keystrokes, form data, or passwords
  • Device identifiers, IP addresses, or telemetry
  • Crash reports or usage analytics

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

TrailGuide Education does not sell, rent, lease, or share personal data with any third parties except as described in this policy (i.e., direct API calls you initiate to providers you configure).

We may disclose data only if required to do so by law or in good-faith belief that such action is necessary to comply with a legal obligation.

Because TrailGuide operates no backend servers and stores no data on our own infrastructure, we have no databases of user data to disclose, breach, or subpoena.

6. Security

We take reasonable technical measures to protect your information:

  • AES-256-GCM encryption for all API keys stored locally, using PBKDF2 key derivation
  • Strict Content Security Policy (CSP) — no inline scripts, no eval, no external script loading except explicitly permitted domains
  • Input sanitization on all user-supplied content before it is processed or displayed
  • Rate limiting (max 10 AI requests per minute) to prevent runaway API usage
  • Minimal permissions — the Extension only requests browser permissions necessary for its features

No system is perfectly secure. If you believe you have found a security vulnerability in TrailGuide, please contact us at the address in Section 10.

8. Your Rights and Choices

Viewing Your Data

All data the Extension stores is in your own browser's storage (chrome.storage). You can inspect it directly in Chrome DevTools → Application → Extension Storage.

Deleting Your Data

You can delete all locally stored data at any time from Options → Danger Zone → Clear All Data. Uninstalling the Extension also removes all local storage.

Revoking Google Drive Access

You can disconnect Google Drive from Options → Google Drive → Disconnect. To fully revoke access, visit myaccount.google.com/permissions and remove TrailGuide.

Disabling Features

You may disable the voice assistant, Google Drive sync, or any AI provider independently from the Options page without affecting other features.

Opting Out of AI Processing

Simply do not use any AI-powered features. The Extension will not send content to any AI provider unless you explicitly trigger a feature.

9. Children's Privacy and K–12 Use

TrailGuide does not verify the age of its users and has no built-in parental-consent mechanism of its own. The Extension is not designed for direct, unsupervised use by children under 13, and is not COPPA-compliant as a standalone consumer product for that age group.

No district data privacy agreement is currently in place As of the effective date of this policy, TrailGuide Education has not entered into a data privacy agreement with any K–12 school district. Individual installation of this Extension by a teacher or student does not create the institutional relationship that COPPA's "school as agent" provision or FERPA's "school official" exception require — both depend on a direct, contracted agreement between the school district and TrailGuide, not on a browser extension being installed independently.

Schools and districts — including those serving students under 13 — should not deploy this Extension for use with student data, and individual educators should not use it with student accounts or student-authored content, until a formal data privacy agreement is in place between their institution and TrailGuide Education. If you administer technology for a school or district and are interested in a formal agreement, contact us using the details in Section 14.

If you believe a child under 13 has used this Extension in a way that transmitted personal data, please contact us so we can address it.

10. FERPA Considerations

Canvas LMS may contain educational records protected by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). When you use TrailGuide's AI features, excerpts of those records are transmitted to the third-party AI provider you have configured (Section 4). TrailGuide does not store these records on its own servers, but this transmission is not automatically covered by any FERPA exception.

The "school official" exception requires a direct agreement FERPA permits a school to share education records with a third party without separate consent only when that party is under the institution's direct control, via a written agreement limiting use to an authorized educational purpose and prohibiting redisclosure. That relationship does not exist by default just because a student or educator has installed this Extension — it requires the institution itself to contract with TrailGuide Education.

If you are an educator or administrator, do not use TrailGuide with FERPA-protected education records unless your institution has such an agreement in place, or has independently determined another applicable basis for the disclosure. Individual, personal use of TrailGuide with your own content is not affected by this section.
